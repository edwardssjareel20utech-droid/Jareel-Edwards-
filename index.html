My Library & Inspiration

These books have significantly influenced my mindset, habits, and financial discipline:

Think and Grow Rich

By Napoleon Hill

Napoleon Hill spent over 20 years studying the wealthiest individuals of his time. He concluded that success is a result of a specific mindset. This book outlines 13 principles, including the power of Desire, Persistence, and the "Mastermind" group.

The core philosophy is that "whatever the mind can conceive and believe, it can achieve," teaching readers to transform thoughts into physical wealth.

Atomic Habits

By James Clear

James Clear demonstrates that massive transformation comes from "atomic" habits—tiny 1% improvements that compound daily. He provides a framework called the Four Laws of Behavior Change: make it obvious, attractive, easy, and satisfying.

By shifting the focus from goals to systems, Clear teaches how to design your environment to make success an inevitable outcome of your routine.

The Richest Man in Babylon

By George S. Clason

Set in ancient Babylon, Clason uses parables to teach timeless financial wisdom. The most famous lesson is to "pay yourself first" by saving at least 10% of everything you earn.

Through the story of Arkad, the book explains how to manage debt, invest wisely, and protect your gold by seeking advice only from those competent to give it.
